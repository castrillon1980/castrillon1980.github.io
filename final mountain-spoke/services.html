The Roots of Dirt.

Some decades ago Men changed the world with new technology. Oh, you’ve heard this before? Well, it wasn’t a computer. It was bikes. Mountain bikes, to be exact. Now, it’s true that people have been riding on rough roads and dusty paths from the earliest days of cycling. But a perfect mix of competition, rapid technology development, and marketing strategy propelled what mountain bike pioneer Charlie Kelly calls a “goofy hobby” into global phenomenon and a new Olympic sport. By the early 1980s, Specialized Bicycles was mass-producing its Stumpjumper mountain bike. Others responded with MTB-specific components and tires. By the end of the decade, many offered bikes with motorcycle-like suspension; disc brakes weren't far behind. In 1996, 20 years after the first Repack, mountain biking debuted at the Atlanta Olympic Games.
